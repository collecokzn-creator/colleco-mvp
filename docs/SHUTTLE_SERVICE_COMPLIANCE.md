## Shuttle Service Requirements Breakdown

### 1. Operating License (Provincial Transport Authority)

**Who needs it**: All shuttle operators charging for passenger transport

**What it authorizes**:
- Specific routes (e.g., OR Tambo Airport ‚Üî Sandton ‚Üî Pretoria)
- Vehicle capacity limits (e.g., 14-seater minibus)
- Operating hours/days
- Type of service (scheduled, charter, door-to-door)

**Common routes for shuttles**:
- Airport transfers (OR Tambo, King Shaka, Cape Town)
- Hotel shuttles (hotel ‚Üî city center)
- Corporate shuttles (office ‚Üî residential areas)
- Event shuttles (conferences, weddings, concerts)

**How to apply**:
1. Submit application to Provincial Transport Authority (per province)
2. Provide business registration, vehicle details, roadworthy certificates
3. Specify routes and operating schedule
4. Pay application fee (R500-2,000 depending on province)
5. Attend public hearing (if required)
6. Processing time: 30-90 days

**Renewal**: Every 1-3 years (varies by province)

**Penalties**: R50,000 fine and/or vehicle impoundment for operating without license

---

### 2. Public Road Passenger Permit (PrDP)

**Who needs it**: All operators carrying paying passengers on public roads

**What it covers**:
- Authorization to charge for passenger transport
- List of registered vehicles
- Passenger capacity per vehicle
- Route/area restrictions

**Difference from Operating License**:
- Operating License = route authorization
- PrDP = permission to charge passengers

**You need BOTH** for legal operation

**How to apply**:
1. Apply to Provincial Operating License Board (POLB)
2. Submit operating license, vehicle registrations, roadworthy certificates
3. Provide driver details with PrDP licenses
4. Submit insurance proof (R10M minimum)
5. Pay application fee (R1,000-3,000)
6. Processing time: 60-120 days

**Renewal**: Every 5 years

---

### 3. Professional Driving Permit (PrDP) - Per Driver

**Who needs it**: Every driver operating a shuttle vehicle

**Requirements**:
- Valid driver's license (Code B minimum, Code C1 recommended for 12-16 seaters)
- Medical certificate (renewable annually for drivers 60+)
- Eye test
- Criminal clearance certificate
- PrDP training course completion (some provinces)

**How to obtain**:
1. Complete driver's license application at licensing office
2. Submit medical certificate and eye test results
3. Provide certified ID copy and criminal clearance
4. Pay fee (R200-500)
5. Processing time: 1-3 days (same as license renewal)

**Validity**: 5 years (same as driver's license)

**License categories needed**:
- **Code B**: Light motor vehicle (cars, small vans)
- **Code C1**: Goods vehicle 3,500-16,000kg OR minibus 12-16 passengers ‚úÖ Recommended
- **Code C**: Heavy goods vehicle >16,000kg OR bus >16 passengers
- **Code EC1**: Code C1 + trailer

**Note**: Most shuttle operators use 12-16 seater minibuses, requiring **Code C1 + PrDP**

---

### 4. Passenger Liability Insurance (R10M minimum)

**Why R10M (not R5M like general partners)**:
- Shuttles carry multiple passengers simultaneously
- Higher risk than single-client services
- Industry standard for passenger transport
- Required by Provincial Operating License Board

**What it covers**:
- Passenger injury or death
- Third-party property damage
- Legal liability
- Medical expenses per passenger
- Excess liability above minimum

**Typical policy structure**:
- Total coverage: R10,000,000
- Per passenger: R500,000
- Third-party property: R2,000,000
- Legal defense: R1,000,000

**Cost**: R3,000-10,000 per vehicle per year (depending on vehicle size, routes, claims history)

**Required details on certificate**:
- Policy number
- Insurer name (Santam, Hollard, Old Mutual, etc.)
- Coverage amount (total and per passenger)
- List of all vehicles on policy
- Effective and expiry dates

**Important**: All vehicles on Operating License and PrDP **must be listed** on insurance policy

---

### 5. Vehicle Roadworthy Certificate (CoF)

**Required for**: Every vehicle on the PrDP

**Validity**: 12 months for commercial vehicles (60 days for new registration)

**What's tested**:
- Brakes, steering, suspension
- Lights, indicators, reflectors
- Tyres (minimum 1.6mm tread depth)
- Windscreen, wipers, mirrors
- Seatbelts (one per seat, must be functional)
- Emergency equipment (fire extinguisher, warning triangle)
- Vehicle structure integrity (no rust, damage)

**How to get**:
1. Book appointment at authorized vehicle testing station
2. Bring vehicle with all safety equipment
3. Test takes 30-60 minutes
4. Certificate issued immediately if passed
5. Retest within 21 days if failed

**Cost**: R500-800 per vehicle

**Renewal**: Annual for commercial vehicles

**Note**: Roadworthy expires faster than driver's license - track carefully!

---

### 6. Vehicle Registration Documents

**Required documents per vehicle**:
- License disk (renewed annually)
- Registration certificate (vehicle ownership proof)
- Proof of owner matching business registration

**Why important**:
- Verifies business owns/leases vehicles legally
- Matches vehicle to Operating License and PrDP
- Confirms license disk is current
- Cross-checks with insurance policy

**Common issues**:
- Vehicle registered to director personally (not business) ‚ö†Ô∏è
- License disk expired ‚ùå
- Vehicle on permit but not registered ‚ùå
- Leased vehicle without proper lease agreement ‚ö†Ô∏è

**Best practice**: Register all shuttle vehicles under business name (Pty Ltd)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Confusing General Liability with Passenger Liability Insurance

‚ùå **Wrong**: "I have R5M public liability insurance"  
‚úÖ **Correct**: "I need R10M passenger liability insurance PLUS R5M general liability"

**Why**: Passenger liability is transport-specific, higher risk coverage

---

### 2. Operating on Unapproved Routes

‚ùå **Wrong**: Operating License says "OR Tambo - Sandton" but doing "Cape Town - Stellenbosch"  
‚úÖ **Correct**: Apply for route amendment before operating new routes

**Penalty**: R50,000 fine and vehicle impoundment

---

### 3. Driver Without PrDP

‚ùå **Wrong**: Driver has Code C1 license (thinks that's enough)  
‚úÖ **Correct**: Driver needs Code C1 **+ PrDP endorsement**

**Penalty**: R5,000 fine per driver, business liable

---

### 4. Expired Roadworthy Certificate

‚ùå **Wrong**: "My roadworthy is 13 months old, still valid for 2 years"  
‚úÖ **Correct**: Commercial vehicles need annual roadworthy renewal

**Penalty**: Cannot operate until renewed, potential insurance claim rejection

---

### 5. Vehicle Not on Insurance Policy

‚ùå **Wrong**: 3 vehicles on permit, only 2 on insurance (to save money)  
‚úÖ **Correct**: ALL vehicles on permit MUST be on insurance

**Risk**: Uninsured vehicle in accident = personal liability (millions)
