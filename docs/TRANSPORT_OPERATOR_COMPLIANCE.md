## South African Transport Regulations

### 1. Operating License (All Paid Passenger Transport)

**Issued by**: Provincial Transport Authority (per province)
**Validity**: 1-3 years (renewable)
**What it covers**:
- Route authorization (specific roads/areas)
- Vehicle capacity limits
- Operating hours/days
- Type of service (scheduled, charter, tourism)

**How to apply**:
1. Complete application form (per province)
2. Submit business registration documents
3. Provide vehicle details and roadworthy certificates
4. Pay application fee (varies by province, ~R500-2000)
5. Attend public hearing (if required)
6. Wait 30-90 days for approval

**Renewal**: Submit 60 days before expiry

---

### 2. Public Road Passenger Permit (PrDP)

**Issued by**: Provincial Operating License Board (POLB)
**Validity**: 5 years (renewable)
**Who needs it**: Any operator charging passengers for road transport
**What it covers**:
- Authorization to carry paying passengers
- List of registered vehicles
- Route/area limits
- Passenger capacity

**How to apply**:
1. Apply to Provincial Operating License Board
2. Submit operating license, vehicle registrations, roadworthy certificates
3. Provide driver details with PrDP licenses
4. Submit insurance proof (R10M minimum)
5. Pay application fee (~R1,000-3,000)
6. Processing time: 60-120 days

**Penalties for non-compliance**: R50,000 fine and/or vehicle impoundment

---

### 3. Tourist Transport Permit (TTP)

**Issued by**: Department of Tourism / Provincial Tourism Board
**Validity**: 2 years (renewable)
**Who needs it**: Tour operators providing transport as part of tourism services
**What it covers**:
- Authorization for tourist-specific transport
- Vehicle branding requirements
- Tourism grading compliance
- Safety standards

**How to apply**:
1. Apply through provincial tourism board
2. Submit SATSA/TGCSA membership proof
3. Provide vehicle details and roadworthy certificates
4. Submit driver details with PrDP
5. Pay application fee (~R2,000-5,000)
6. Processing time: 30-60 days

**Format**: TTP-[YEAR]-[NUMBER] (e.g., TTP-2024-5678)

---

### 4. Professional Driving Permit (PrDP) - Per Driver

**Issued by**: Department of Transport (licensing offices)
**Validity**: 5 years (renewable)
**Who needs it**: All drivers of vehicles carrying paying passengers
**Requirements**:
- Valid driver's license (minimum Code B, typically Code C1 or EC1 for minibuses)
- Medical certificate (renewable annually for drivers 60+)
- Eye test
- Clearance certificate (criminal record check)
- PrDP training course completion

**How to apply**:
1. Complete driver's license application form
2. Submit medical certificate and eye test results
3. Provide certified ID copy
4. Submit criminal clearance certificate
5. Complete PrDP training (if required)
6. Pay fee (~R200-500)
7. Processing time: 1-3 days (same as license renewal)

**License categories**:
- **Code C1**: Goods vehicle 3,500-16,000kg OR minibus 12-16 passengers
- **Code C**: Heavy goods vehicle >16,000kg OR bus >16 passengers
- **Code EC1**: Code C1 + trailer
- **Code EC**: Code C + trailer

**Penalties**: R5,000 fine for operating without PrDP

---

### 5. Vehicle Roadworthy Certificate (CoF)

**Issued by**: Authorized vehicle testing stations
**Validity**: 60 days for new registration, 12 months for renewals
**Who needs it**: All vehicles used for commercial passenger transport
**What's tested**:
- Brakes, steering, suspension
- Lights, indicators, reflectors
- Tyres, windscreen, wipers
- Seatbelts (one per seat)
- Emergency equipment (fire extinguisher, warning triangle)
- Vehicle structure integrity

**How to get**:
1. Book appointment at authorized testing station
2. Bring vehicle with all safety equipment
3. Test takes 30-60 minutes
4. Certificate issued on the spot if passed
5. Retest within 21 days if failed

**Cost**: R500-800 per vehicle

**Renewal**: Annual for commercial vehicles

---

### 6. Passenger Liability Insurance

**Minimum coverage**: R10,000,000 (R10M) for transport operators
**What it covers**:
- Passenger injury or death
- Third-party property damage
- Legal liability
- Excess liability above minimum

**Required details**:
- Policy number
- Insurer name (Santam, Hollard, Old Mutual, etc.)
- Coverage amount (total and per passenger)
- List of all vehicles on policy
- Effective and expiry dates

**Recommended insurers for transport operators**:
- Santam Commercial
- Hollard Insurance
- Old Mutual Insure
- Bryte Insurance
- Constantia Insurance

**Cost**: R3,000-10,000 per vehicle per year (depending on vehicle size, routes, claims history)

---

### 7. Cross-Border Permit (International Tours)

**Issued by**: Cross-Border Road Transport Agency (CBRTA)
**Validity**: 2 years (renewable)
**Who needs it**: Operators traveling to Botswana, Zimbabwe, Mozambique, Namibia, Lesotho, eSwatini
**What it covers**:
- Authorization for cross-border passenger transport
- Customs clearance facilitation
- Vehicle authorization (per vehicle)
- Country-specific approvals

**How to apply**:
1. Apply to CBRTA (online or in person)
2. Submit operating license and PrDP
3. Provide vehicle registrations and roadworthy certificates
4. Submit passport copies of directors
5. Provide insurance covering all SADC countries
6. Pay application fee (~R5,000-10,000 per vehicle)
7. Processing time: 60-90 days

**SADC countries requiring permits**:
- Botswana
- Zimbabwe
- Mozambique
- Namibia
- Lesotho
- eSwatini (Swaziland)

**Note**: Some countries have bilateral agreements allowing easier access (e.g., SA-Botswana)

## Automated Verification Workflow

### Step 1: Document Upload (Partner Self-Service)

Partner uploads all required documents via **PartnerVerification** page:
- Standard documents (business reg, tax, insurance, ID, banking)
- Transport-specific documents (operating license, permits, driver licenses, roadworthy certs)

### Step 2: Azure OCR Extraction (R0.027/doc)

For each document:
- Extract all text using Azure Form Recognizer
- Parse permit numbers, expiry dates, vehicle registrations, names
- Flag documents with:
  - ‚ùå Expired or expiring within 60 days
  - ‚ùå Missing required fields (e.g., no permit number)
  - ‚ùå Low confidence OCR (blurry, damaged documents)

## Compliance Checklist for Admins

### Before Approving Transport Operator:

- [ ] All vehicles on Operating License match PrDP Permit
- [ ] All vehicles on permits listed on insurance policy
- [ ] Insurance coverage ‚â• R10M (not R5M)
- [ ] Each driver has valid PrDP for correct vehicle category
- [ ] All roadworthy certificates valid (not expired)
- [ ] All license disks valid (check vehicle registration docs)
- [ ] Operating routes cover all advertised destinations
- [ ] Cross-border permit present if offering international tours
- [ ] Business registered and tax compliant
- [ ] Trade license valid (SATSA/TGCSA if applicable)

### Red Flags (Auto-Reject or Manual Review):

- ‚ùå Expired permits or licenses (any document)
- ‚ùå Driver PrDP category doesn't match vehicle type
- ‚ùå Insurance doesn't cover all vehicles
- ‚ùå Missing roadworthy for any vehicle
- ‚ùå Vehicle on permit not on insurance policy
- ‚ùå Operating routes don't include advertised destinations
- ‚ùå Missing cross-border permit but offering international tours
- ‚ùå PrDP vehicles don't match operating license vehicles
